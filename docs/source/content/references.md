## Arctic sea ice — model and reanalysis references

- Sakov, P. et al. (2012). [TOPAZ4: An ocean-sea ice data assimilation system for the
  North Atlantic and Arctic](https://doi.org/10.5194/os-8-633-2012). *Ocean Science*, 8(4).

- Schweiger, A. et al. (2011). [Uncertainty in modeled Arctic sea ice volume](https://doi.org/10.1029/2011JC007084).
  *Journal of Geophysical Research: Oceans*, 116(C8). *(PIOMAS sea ice volume reanalysis.)*

- Nerger, L. & Hiller, W. (2013). [Software for ensemble-based data assimilation
  systems — implementation strategies and scalability](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cageo.2013.06.003).
  *Computers & Geosciences*, 55. *(TOPAZ4 DA system — PDAF.)*

## Sea ice observations

- Spreen, G., Kaleschke, L. & Heygster, G. (2008). [Sea ice remote sensing using AMSR-E
  89-GHz channels](https://doi.org/10.1029/2005JC003384). *Journal of Geophysical Research:
  Oceans*, 113(C2). *(ASI algorithm used by the AMSR2 SIC product.)*

- Rigor, I. G. (2002). [IABP drifting buoy pressure, temperature, position, and
  interpolated ice velocity](https://doi.org/10.7265/N53X84K7). NSIDC Special Report.
  *(IABP dataset used as in-situ reference.)*

- Röhrs, J. & Kaleschke, L. (2012). [An algorithm to detect sea ice leads by using
  AMSR-E passive microwave imagery](https://doi.org/10.5194/tc-6-343-2012).
  *The Cryosphere*, 6(2). *(Background for lead-map products.)*

- Lindsay, R. W. & Rothrock, D. A. (1995). [Arctic sea ice leads from advanced very high
  resolution radiometer images](https://doi.org/10.1029/94JC02393). *Journal of Geophysical
  Research: Oceans*, 100(C3). *(MODIS lead-map context.)*

## ML for sea ice prediction

- Kim, Y. J. et al. (2020). [Prediction of Arctic sea ice concentration using a fully
  data driven deep neural network](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.rse.2020.111819).
  *Remote Sensing of Environment*, 246.

- Andersson, T. R. et al. (2021). [Seasonal Arctic sea ice forecasting with probabilistic
  deep learning](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-021-25257-4). *Nature Communications*, 12.

- Horvath, S. et al. (2020). [Predicting the evolution of sea ice leads using machine
  learning](https://doi.org/10.5194/tc-14-3455-2020). *The Cryosphere*, 14(10).

- Mu, B. et al. (2023). [A deep learning model for forecasting global monthly mean
  sea surface temperature anomalies](https://doi.org/10.1029/2022MS003137).
  *Journal of Advances in Modeling Earth Systems*, 15. *(Relevant ML forecasting context.)*
